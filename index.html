Our Story

From the Mountains of Sana'a to Den Haag

Yemen Palace was born from a deep love for the food that brought our family together around the sufra — the communal dining cloth. Our founder, originally from Sana'a, carried grandmother's recipes across the world and opened this restaurant with one promise: that every guest would feel at home.

In Yemeni culture, hospitality (diyafa) is not simply good manners — it is a sacred duty. We prepare every dish as if cooking for family: with patience, love, and the finest spices sourced directly from Yemeni markets.

The Experience

More Than a Meal

Dining at Yemen Palace is a journey — into culture, warmth, and flavours that tell a story.

🨙
Authentic Recipes

Every recipe has been passed down through generations. We use traditional cooking methods including underground tandoor roasting and slow simmering in clay pots — the same way Yemen's grandmothers cook today.

🤝
Communal Dining

In Yemen, food is always shared. Our large shared platters and traditional sufra style encourage you to gather, bond, and feast together. We welcome families, groups, and celebrations of every kind.

✨
Warm Hospitality

Diyafa — Yemeni hospitality — is at the heart of everything we do. Every guest is greeted with fresh dates, Arabic coffee, and the genuine warmth that makes Yemen legendary for its welcome.

Qishr — Yemen's Sacred Coffee
Coffee & Tea Culture

The Ritual of Qishr

Yemeni coffee is unlike any other in the world. Qishr is brewed not from the bean itself, but from the golden coffee husk — spiced with fresh ginger and green cardamom, giving it a uniquely smooth and warming character.

In Yemen, a cup of Qishr is never just a drink. It is an invitation — to slow down, to converse, to welcome. We serve it the traditional way, poured from a dallah pot into small ceramic cups with no handles.

Pair it with our warm bint al-sahn — layered honeyed bread — for a truly authentic Yemeni welcome experience.
